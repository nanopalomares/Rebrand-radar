Rebrand Radar

Brands are always changing.
We've never been able to see it.

Every enterprise customer we have will rebrand. Most of their competitors will too. And right now, we find out by accident. On a call, through LinkedIn, or after the window has already closed. This is our proposal to fix that, and why the data it generates could matter for Team Studio too.

The problem

We are finding out too late, or not at all.

AEs and CSMs discover rebrands by chance. There is no system. When we do find out, it is usually after the rebrand has already happened. By then, the deals have stalled or someone else is in the room.

The rebrand lifecycle

There are three phases. We only show up in one.

The average rebrand touches 215 assets over 7 months. Where we get in changes everything, and right now we're getting in at the worst possible time.

Phase What is happening Who is in the room Canva today
A. Planning Agency, vision, strategy. Happens before anything goes public. Adobe, agencies, existing vendors Hard to get into
B. Staggered rollout Assets go live one by one across every channel and market. Pain is live and current. 215 assets, 7 months. Wide open Our window. This is where Radar gets us in.
C. Post refresh Rebrand is done. Teams drowning in stale assets and backlogs. Already too late Reactive. The deal has moved on.
The idea

Rebrand Radar. An agent that watches so we do not have to.

We scan all 16,700 enterprise customers and 2 to 3 of their competitors every week across every public touchpoint: websites, LinkedIn, socials, ads. That is a pool of over 50,000 brands in constant motion. When Radar detects a signal, it triggers deep research and routes a ready-to-use brief to the right AE or CSM before anyone else knows.

What we are watching

50,000+

brand touchpoints scanned weekly. 16,700 enterprise customers plus 2 to 3 competitors each. Websites, LinkedIn, socials, ads. Every channel, every market.

Why it matters at this scale

Brands rebrand every 7 to 10 years and do smaller refreshes every 3 to 5 years. Across 16,700 accounts, something is always changing. We have just never had a way to see it or act on it.

How it works
1
Weekly scanning
All touchpoints checked. LinkedIn picks up signals 1 to 2 weeks before websites update.
2
Scoring and deduplication
Every change scored by significance. Noise filtered out before it reaches anyone.
3
Deep research
Customer rebrand or competitor rebrand. Two tracks, tailored research for each.
4
Human review
Every brief reviewed by AE or CSM before anything is sent. No automated outreach.
5
Brief delivered
Slack alert plus ready-to-use brief routed to the right person at the right time.

For Team Studio

Here is how we think the data could be useful for Team Studio.

Radar is not just a sales tool. The data it generates, across 50,000 brand touchpoints every week, could offer some interesting signals. Here is what we think could be worth exploring together.

7–10 yr
Major full rebrand cycle for most companies (Bynder, Erahaus, Crowdspring, 2025 to 2026)
16,700 accounts divided by 7 years = ~2,385 major rebrands per year across our customer base alone. That is roughly 45 per week.
3–5 yr
Smaller brand refreshes: typography, colour palettes, visual updates
16,700 accounts divided by 3 years = ~5,565 brand refreshes per year. That is over 100 per week. All creating template demand.
01
Template demand, predicted
Know what is needed before customers ask.
215 assets per rebrand Every rebrand touches 215 assets over 7 months. Every asset is a template need. Radar detects the rebrand early, so there could be an opportunity to build suites proactively before the request ever lands. Instead of reactive production, you are anticipating demand.
02
Industry trend signals
When a whole sector shifts, you will see it first.
Sector-wide pattern detection When multiple companies in the same industry are rebranding at the same time, think a sector responding to a market shift. That is a design trend signal. That data could inform what template families get prioritised in discovery and search before the trend is widely visible.
03
Template freshness, automated
Know when your templates are going stale.
Continuous brand auditing Radar audits how a brand looks across all its public touchpoints continuously. That same logic could flag when Canva templates no longer reflect how a brand actually looks today. Instead of waiting for a customer to complain, you would know first.
04
Cross-market consistency gaps
Is Pizza Hut Italy running the same brand as Pizza Hut US?
Every market, every channel Radar checks whether a brand looks consistent across markets and channels. Every inconsistency it finds is a signal about where scalable template systems could close the gap. That feels like exactly the kind of thing Team Studio could do something useful with.
